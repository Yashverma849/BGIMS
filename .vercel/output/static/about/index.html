MM BGIMS/About
Our Heritage

Twenty-three years
of quiet
excellence.

At MM BGIMS, we measure ourselves not by rankings or rhetoric, but by the trajectories of the women and men who have walked through our halls — and by the seriousness with which we hold ourselves to the work of management education.

Chapter 01
The Beginning

Founded in 2002 under the Maratha Mandir Trust — an institution itself older than the Republic.

When the institute opened its doors in the corridors of the Maratha Mandir Annexe at Mumbai Central, the country was still finding its post-liberalisation feet. India needed managers — but more than that, it needed a generation of practitioners who understood the marriage of tradition and ambition that defines our commerce.

The vision was singular: to build a school close enough to the markets to be useful, distant enough from fashion to be wise. From a single MMS programme, we have grown to four flagship offerings — but the founding instinct has not changed.

Chapter 02
The Discipline

A philosophy of teaching that is classical in its rigour and contemporary in its concerns.

We believe management education is not a vocational programme. It is a humanistic enterprise — concerned, finally, with the question of how we live and work together. Our curricula are deliberately structured: hard quantitative foundations, then case-driven application, then the slow reckoning with ethics and ambiguity that distinguishes a manager from a calculator.

Our faculty teach with the temperament of practitioners. Our visiting faculty arrive from the boardrooms of Reliance, the trading desks of HDFC, and the law offices of Cyril Amarchand. The classroom is our laboratory — and the laboratory takes itself seriously.

Chapter 03
The Tomorrow

Looking forward — to a campus, a curriculum, and a community equal to the next two decades of Indian commerce.

As India approaches its centenary as a republic, the demands placed on our managers will only grow more complex. We are responding — with a renewed Banking & Finance MBA, a doctoral programme that publishes in the world's leading journals, and partnerships with universities in Singapore, London, and Boston.

But the ambition is constant: to graduate men and women who, when their hour comes, are equipped to meet it.

Our Compass

The principles that orient our work.

i.

Vision

To be among India's most respected business schools — not by size, but by the substance of our scholarship and the calibre of our alumni.

ii.

Mission

To deliver a management education that integrates analytical rigour, ethical seriousness, and a deep familiarity with the practice of Indian commerce.

iii.

Values

Curiosity. Humility. Industriousness. The conviction that good management is, at its root, a moral undertaking.

A Brief Chronology

Twenty-three years, in milestones.

2002

Foundation

The Institute is established under the Maratha Mandir Trust at the historic Mumbai Central campus. Inaugural MMS cohort of forty.

2008

First Accreditation

AICTE approval secured. The institute is granted full affiliation with the University of Mumbai.

2014

MBA Banking & Finance

The flagship Banking & Finance MBA programme is launched, designed in consultation with senior practitioners from RBI and SBI.

2018

Doctoral Programme

University of Mumbai grants Ph.D. recognition. First cohort of doctoral fellows is admitted.

2022

NAAC B++

National accreditation council confers B++ grade — placing the institute in the top tier of Mumbai's business schools.

2025

Twenty-Third Cohort

More than two thousand alumni now serve in the leadership of India's most consequential firms.

The Campus

An unhurried place to study,
in the heart of an unhurriable city.

The Library

Twenty-eight thousand volumes. Subscriptions to JSTOR, EBSCO, Bloomberg Terminal, and the Harvard Business Review archive — open to all students.

The Auditorium

Two-hundred-seat capacity, with a programme of guest lectures featuring sitting CEOs, Reserve Bank governors past, and visiting scholars from London and Singapore.

Bloomberg Lab

A dedicated finance & analytics lab with twelve Bloomberg terminals, Eikon, and Reuters subscriptions — used by both finance students and doctoral fellows.

Entrepreneurship Cell

A working incubator. Six student-led ventures since 2020, with seed funding partnerships through the Mumbai Angels network.
